Project Overview

This repository contains my Master's project analyzing the relationship between various weather parameters and triathlon race performance. The study examines how factors such as temperature, humidity, wind speed, and other meteorological conditions affect athlete performance in endurance sports.

Methodology

📈 Performance Analysis

Analyzed weather effects across different race distances, athlete ability levels, and environmental conditions.

Key Research Findings

🌤️ Weather Dominance

Weather variables have 3-5 times greater impact than elevation features across all race segments. Temperature emerged as the dominant predictor with optimal performance at:

  • Air temperature: 19-27°C
  • Water temperature: 20-24°C

🤖 Model Performance

XGBoost models demonstrated superior predictive power:

  • Swim segments: R² = 0.91
  • Bike segments: R² = 0.63
  • Run segments: R² = 0.68
  • Overall times: R² = 0.61

👨‍🏃‍♂️ Athlete Categories

Professional vs. Age-group differences:

  • Professionals: 30% less sensitivity to temperature extremes
  • Stronger correlations between environmental factors and professional performance
  • Age-group athletes more vulnerable to heat stress

🏁 Race Format Differences

Half Ironman vs. Full Ironman impacts:

  • Half Ironman: Higher weather-to-elevation ratios
  • More intense environmental effects per distance unit
  • Different optimal temperature ranges by format

🔍 Quantitative Impact Analysis

  • Temperature Effect: Every 1°C rise increases run time by ~0.91 minutes (54.65 seconds)
  • Elevation Impact: Bike time increases by ~0.24 seconds per meter of elevation gain
  • WBGT Correlation: Every 1 unit increase in WBGT adds 1.30 minutes to run time
  • Weather vs Elevation Ratios: 12.3:1 (Half Ironman swim), 6.1:1 (Full Ironman swim)

Research Impact & Applications

This analysis reveals significant relationships between weather parameters and triathlon performance, with practical implications for multiple stakeholders:

🏁 Race Organizers

  • Optimal Scheduling: Evidence-based timing for peak performance conditions
  • Safety Protocols: WBGT-based risk assessment thresholds
  • Course Design: Balancing elevation with climate considerations
  • Resource Planning: Enhanced cooling strategies for high-risk conditions

🏃‍♂️ Athletes & Coaches

  • Performance Prediction: Data-driven race strategy development
  • Training Optimization: Climate-specific preparation protocols
  • Race Selection: Strategic event choosing based on environmental sensitivity
  • Heat Adaptation: Targeted acclimatization programs

📊 Sports Science

  • Predictive Models: Advanced machine learning applications
  • Environmental Research: Comprehensive weather impact quantification
  • Performance Analysis: Segment-specific environmental effects
  • Comparative Studies: Format-specific environmental responses

⚠️ Health & Safety

  • Risk Assessment: Demographic-specific vulnerability identification
  • Heat Illness Prevention: Evidence-based safety thresholds
  • Medical Planning: Environmental condition-based resource allocation
  • Education Programs: Athlete awareness and preparation strategies

🎯 Key Practical Insights

  • Gender Differences: Female athletes show greater sensitivity to humidity and temperature variations
  • Experience Matters: Professional athletes demonstrate 30% less sensitivity to temperature extremes
  • Format Effects: Half Ironman events show amplified environmental impacts per distance unit
  • Optimal Conditions: Peak performance occurs at 19-27°C air temperature and 20-24°C water temperature
